Step 1 · Foundation

Get Set Up with Claude

Download Claude and install it on your desktop. Sign up for Claude Pro, finish setup, then open Claude Co-work — your AI-powered workspace for finance and beyond.

  • Download Claude and install the desktop app
  • Sign up for Claude Pro (required for Co-work)
  • Finish Claude setup and log in
  • Open Claude Co-work — must have Claude Pro to access
02
Step 2 · Setup

Organise Your Files

Set up your folder structure on your desktop so everything stays organised and easy to find when working with Claude.

  • Create a folder called Claude on your desktop
  • Inside it, create a file called Personal Finance Tracker
  • Create a separate file for each card you want to track finances for
  • Save your Excel template into the Claude folder

Step 3 · Data

Download & Redact Your Bank Statements

Download PDFs from your bank for each respective card. Before uploading anything, redact your vital personal information to protect your privacy.

  • Download PDFs from your bank for each card you want to track
  • Open each PDF in the Mac Preview app
  • Use the Markup toolbar and select the Redact tool
  • Manually redact sensitive details — account numbers, sort codes, personal addresses

Step 4 · Budget

Build Your Monthly Budget

With your transaction data loaded, ask Claude to categorize your spending and build a budget based on your actual habits — not a generic template. Claude will separate fixed costs from variable spending and surface where money is quietly leaking.

Try this prompt

"Based on my transactions, categorize my spending into fixed costs, variable necessities, and discretionary. Then suggest a realistic monthly budget that moves me toward my goals."

05
Step 5 · Tracking

Track Daily Expenses

Build a weekly rhythm of pasting new transactions into Claude and asking for a running budget update. Claude flags categories trending over budget before the month ends — giving you time to adjust rather than just reporting damage after the fact.

  • Set a 10-minute "finance check-in" every Sunday evening
  • Ask Claude to highlight unusual or one-off charges
  • Ask Claude to surface forgotten subscriptions each month
06
Step 6 · Analysis

Analyze Spending Patterns

After 2–3 months of data, Claude can surface patterns you'd never catch manually — which days you tend to overspend, which categories creep up mid-month, and where small purchases are quietly eroding your budget over time.

Try this prompt

"Look at my last 3 months of spending. What are my top 3 problem areas? Are there any recurring patterns in when or why I overspend? What single habit change would have the biggest impact?"

07
Step 7 · Savings

Create a Savings Plan

Claude builds a savings strategy around your actual income cycle — bi-weekly paychecks, freelance payments, or irregular cash flow. It models multiple savings rates and shows exactly how long each goal takes to reach under each scenario.

  • Ask Claude to model conservative, moderate, and aggressive scenarios
  • Set up named "buckets" for each savings goal (emergency, travel, etc.)
  • Schedule automatic transfers the day after payday to remove temptation
08
Step 8 · Debt

Manage Debt Strategically

Claude calculates exact payoff timelines for the avalanche method (highest interest first) vs snowball method (smallest balance first), and can find a hybrid that maximizes both mathematical savings and psychological momentum.

Try this prompt

"I have: Visa $4,200 at 22% APR, student loan $18,000 at 5.5%, car loan $9,500 at 7.9%. I can put $800/month toward debt. Show me avalanche vs snowball with exact payoff dates and total interest paid."

09
Step 9 · Investing

Get Investment Guidance

Once your budget produces a consistent surplus, Claude explains investment vehicles in plain language matched to your situation — index funds, ETFs, tax-advantaged accounts (TFSA/RRSP in Canada, 401k/IRA in the US) — and compares specific funds by cost and structure.

  • Always max tax-advantaged accounts before taxable investing
  • Ask Claude to explain any term before you commit to a product
  • Use Claude to compare funds by MER or expense ratio side-by-side
  • Ask about your country-specific rules — Claude knows both US and CA tax law
10
Step 10 · Automate

Run Your Monthly Review

The final step is turning this into a rhythm. At the start of each month, open Claude and run your review. Paste last month's transactions, compare to your budget, update your goals, and set three intentions for the month ahead. Twenty minutes. Total clarity.

Your monthly ritual prompt

"Here are my transactions for [month]. Compare to my budget, calculate my savings rate, update my debt payoff timeline, and give me my three financial priorities for next month."
